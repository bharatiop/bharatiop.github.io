    <span class="eyebrow">Focus Areas</span>
    <h2 class="section-title">Our research themes</h2>
  </div>

  <div class="research-focus-grid">

    <div class="research-focus-card">
      <div class="research-focus-icon">⭐</div>
      <h3>Equation of State of Dense Matter</h3>
      <p>
        The equation of state (EoS) — how pressure relates to density — governs every observable property of a neutron star. We develop relativistic mean-field (RMF) models calibrated to nuclear laboratory data, then confront them with astrophysical observations: LIGO tidal deformability, NICER X-ray mass-radius measurements, and pulsar maximum masses. Key models we work with include the NITR EoS family, G3, IOPB-I, and BigApple parameter sets.
      </p>
      <div class="tags">
        <span class="tag">RMF Theory</span>
        <span class="tag">NICER</span>
        <span class="tag">LIGO Constraints</span>
        <span class="tag">Nuclear Saturation</span>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="research-focus-card">
      <div class="research-focus-icon">🔮</div>
      <h3>Hyperons, Delta-Baryons &amp; Exotic Matter</h3>
      <p>
        Beyond protons and neutrons, the cores of massive neutron stars may harbour strange baryons (Λ, Σ, Ξ hyperons), delta-baryons (Δ), or even deconfined quark matter. We investigate how these exotic degrees of freedom soften or stiffen the EoS, their role in the "hyperon puzzle" (tension between softening and 2M☉ observations), and twin-star signatures from strong first-order phase transitions.
      </p>
      <div class="tags">
        <span class="tag">Hyperon Puzzle</span>
        <span class="tag">Delta-Baryons</span>
        <span class="tag">Quarkyonic Matter</span>
        <span class="tag">Phase Transitions</span>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="research-focus-card">
      <div class="research-focus-icon">🌊</div>
      <h3>Gravitational Waves &amp; Stellar Oscillations</h3>
      <p>
        Oscillating neutron stars emit continuous gravitational waves. We compute quasinormal mode frequencies (f-modes, p-modes, w-modes) in full general relativity, and derive universal I-Love-Q and oscillation–tidal-deformability relations that are insensitive to the EoS. These provide EoS-independent tests with next-generation detectors (Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) and are directly comparable to post-merger GW signals.
      </p>
      <div class="tags">
        <span class="tag">f-modes / p-modes</span>
        <span class="tag">I-Love-Q</span>
        <span class="tag">Tidal Deformability</span>
        <span class="tag">Einstein Telescope</span>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="research-focus-card">
      <div class="research-focus-icon">🌑</div>
      <h3>Dark Matter in Neutron Stars</h3>
      <p>
        Neutron stars can capture and accumulate dark matter over their lifetime. We model the effect of WIMP dark matter, fermionic dark matter, and self-interacting dark matter on neutron star structure through two-fluid TOV equations. Observables — maximum mass, radius, tidal deformability, radial oscillation frequencies — shift in characteristic ways that constrain the dark matter–nucleon cross-section and dark matter particle mass. We also connect to PSR J0952-0607 (the heaviest known pulsar) and HESS J1731-347 constraints.
      </p>
      <div class="tags">
        <span class="tag">WIMP Dark Matter</span>
        <span class="tag">Fermionic DM</span>
        <span class="tag">Two-fluid TOV</span>
        <span class="tag">PSR J0952-0607</span>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="research-focus-card">
      <div class="research-focus-icon">🌡</div>
      <h3>Thermal Evolution &amp; Proto-Neutron Stars</h3>
      <p>
        Newly born proto-neutron stars are hot (~50 MeV) and lepton-rich. We study how finite temperature and trapped neutrinos modify the EoS, alter composition, and affect macroscopic structure. Our work also covers neutron star cooling: the Urca processes, modified Urca, pair-breaking-formation processes, and how they constrain the density-dependence of nuclear symmetry energy and the gap energies of superfluid phases.
      </p>
      <div class="tags">
        <span class="tag">Proto-NS</span>
        <span class="tag">Finite Temperature EoS</span>
        <span class="tag">NS Cooling</span>
        <span class="tag">Urca Process</span>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="research-focus-card">
      <div class="research-focus-icon">🔭</div>
      <h3>Multi-Messenger Astrophysics</h3>
      <p>
        The 2017 neutron star merger GW170817 ushered in the era of multi-messenger nuclear astrophysics. We use simultaneous GW + EM constraints to infer the EoS, tidal deformability, moment of inertia, and nuclear symmetry energy parameters. We also study how future observations with LIGO-India, AstroSat, and the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) can disentangle different EoS models and detect exotic phases of matter.
      </p>
